A Hackitivist Representation Professor Brian Lonsway
Undergraduate Thesis
 Growing up with the Internet, I have always been perplexed about the mysterious, yet, ubiquitous world of the Internet. This thesis is motivated by such curiosity through a playful process of uncovering the dual (physical and virtual) nature of the Internet, Using auto CAD as a medium in which to explore such duality, this thesis looks into synthesizing the spatial structures in which we navigate the physical and virtual world. It iterates ways of representing the mediation between the two worlds. These representations are prototypes that the architect can use to better understand relationships between the two worlds as a critical part of architectural design. They aspire to aid the architect in developing agency over the design process in a world that is becoming ever more interconnected with the exponential rise of IoT (the Internet of things).

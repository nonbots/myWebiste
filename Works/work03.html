The proposal revolves around the strict and logical process of aggregated primitive forms to create larger and varied forms. In order to meet the practicalities of the real estateeconomy in West Chelsea, the abstract and scaleless form was sectioned and extrudedand and then inserted into the real built environment.The project becomes two worlds: the underneath world that serves the public andthe above world that serves the built world. At the ground level, the form is a unique elaborated cave-like surface serving theartistic culture of West Chelsea and transforms into fringe like apertures serving as condo and office spaces with views facing towards the Hudson River and the Highline at the top.From the Highline, the project can be viewed as an art piece that performs throughout the day as it interacts with the light and the shadows projected onto its surface.
